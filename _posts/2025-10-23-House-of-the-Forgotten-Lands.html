GEOSINT Challenge: House of the Forgotten Lands

Challenge Description

In the quiet stretches of forgotten lands, some places linger, unnoticed and untouched, as though time itself has chosen to bypass them. A humble dwelling, nestled in a corner of the world that few travelers ever venture to. At first glance, it appears to be nothing more than an ordinary house, weathered by time, blending into its surroundings as if it were part of the very earth itself.

But as you begin to search, something feels… off. The house, though unremarkable on the surface, seems to hold secrets hidden beneath its foundation. A faint sense of magic lingers in the air, barely perceptible, but undeniable. Why has this house remained so inconspicuous in a world full of grand castles, mythical creatures, and ancient ruins? What is its purpose, and who—or what—might have called it home?

The more you uncover, the deeper the mystery grows. The trail is faint, the records scarce, but you cannot shake the feeling that this house holds a story—a story buried deep within its walls, waiting to be revealed.

And as you continue your search, following the scattered clues, you might find yourself thinking: "After all this, I could really use a good soak in a nearby hot tub." Whether it’s the slow pace of discovery or the mystery itself, sometimes the greatest rewards are found in the simplest of comforts.

Good luck uncovering the mysteries of the House of the Forgotten Lands.

Analysis

Most of the description is just fun little background for a challenge that doesn't really provide anything useful to the challenge however there is one line specifically that should be considered a hint (more on this later).

Where is the Sun?

Some things that need to be taken into consideration is the direction of the house is facing. Though it is difficult to tell we are able to faintly see the shadows of the trees. This tells us that the sun is behind the camera somewhere on the right side. While the image is well lit, it doesn't seem to be the brightest it could be if it were mid day, so we can assume its before mid day. If we take both of these into consideration we can reasonably argue that the house is facing South as the sun is yet to reach it's highest point and its path from our perspective is right to left or in other words East to West.

Knowing the house is facing South with the sun rising from the East from behind our perspective, we can narrow it down to the Northern Hemisphere.

What brand is that caravan?

Next we can take notice of the caravan next to the house. If we give the image to Google Images and focus on the caravan we get quite a few results leading back to the brand, Knaus.

Knaus being a German brand allows to shift our prospective closer to Europe.

The Hill

Nothing really to point out about the hill besides the fact that you know the hill is on the right and will help you line up the house. I guess you could have used a topographic map looking for villages that have a hill on the East side.

Honestly I choose this specific location for 2 reasons. I randomly spun google maps a ton and zoomed in on a single location and I really liked the hill. Gave me Windows XP Bliss vibes.

The Hot Tub

After all this, I could really use a good soak in a nearby hot tub.

This line alluded to a nearby guesthouse that mentions free hot tub. This in my opinion is the biggest give away before the extra hints. However this alone does not provide us much detail on the actual location of the house.

There is a hot tub nearby!

The House

We can clearly see that the house has a number on it, 5.

Congrats that's ⅓ of the flag!

Seriously though, we know the house is visible via street view. The style is not typical for a Western Style house. This further points it closer to the East.

Hint #1 (or 2 if you consider the hot tub line a hint)

The house is rumored to be in lands covered in ice. With a measly population that numbers less than 200.

This hint basically just tells you it's Iceland as well as it's a very small town.

Using this information you could have used the Locations of Iceland wiki page to form a list of villages and settlements to narrow your search.

Hint #2 (or 3)

The street name sounds/looks like it could be a name for a dragon.

I will admit this one is pretty subjective, however I personally find that the street name, Smaragrund, to sound as if it were taken straight from a fictional story. It appears to be a combination of the Icelandic words, smaragd meaning emerald and grund meaning ground. Essentially meaning Emerald Ground.

This makes me think of Smaug from J.R.R Tolkien's The Hobbit. Thus I imagine a Emerald Green dragon with the power to shake the ground, named Smaragrund.

I guess my hint should have included the either of these words: Emerald or Green. So I apologize as this hint was not very good.

Flagging

Using a combination of information gathered from the Analysis (and the hints if these were used), you would have eventually gotten to Laugarbakki, Iceland. Using the satellite view, you would eventually find our House of the Forgotten Lands!
