Challenge Description

Intel has intercept that XYZ bad guy group has cargo coming in that contains biological warfare weaponry that we need to stop. We have found an image of the 4 cargo ships meeting up with 2 ships to transfer the weapon over. However our inside source was silenced before they could tell us where it was taken. We need you to figure out where the handoff took place so we can dig up some dirt from the locals.

Analysis

Upon the initial analysis of the image, we can see what appears to be a setting sun, 4 cargo ships sailing from left to right, 2 sailboats sailing from right to left, some rocks in the water, and grass.

Where is the Sun?

With what appears to be the sun setting we can reasonably assume that the left side of the image is actually West. This allows us to determine that our perspective is facing North.

Knowing the sun is setting allows us to determine that we are facing North. This would mean that this image was most likely taken in the Southern Hemisphere

What about the ships?

We can take notice that the ships are close enough to look decently sized in the photo but still far away enough that it does not appear they are preparing to dock here. This likely means they are sailing past which should lead us to thinking we are on an island.

If we are indeed on an island, in the Southern Hemisphere, this is extremely helpful for narrowing down potential locations, I would say about 20 locations or so.

The rocks

We can clearly see that there are black volcanic rocks in the water just off the coastline. This further strengthens our lead of it being an island specifically one of volcanic origin.

We are almost 100% sure we are on an island in the Southern Hemisphere of volcanic origin.

Hint #1

Investigate southern remote islands along quiet trade routes known for cargo handoffs away from surveillance.

This just straight up tells us we are in the Southern Hemisphere.

Using this information you could have used the Island countries or territories section in the Southern Hemisphere wiki to form a list of potential islands to narrow our search.

Hint #2

The first time our contact was willing to meet us, required us to meet at the only town in the area.

This would narrow it down to islands that have only 1 main town/city.

The flag format also alludes to this by using the word seat instead of town.

Flagging

Using a combination of information gathered from the Analysis (and the hints if these were used), you would have found Hanga Roa, Easter Island. Simply using street view along the coastline we can find the exact spot to confirm that this is the correct location.

The Hanga Roa wiki provides you with all the information required for the flag. Seat in this case would be the administrative center or capital of that island.
